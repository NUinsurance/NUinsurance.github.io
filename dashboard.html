What are the differences between premium and deductible?
Premium is the amount of money you have to pay for insurance. Premiums are usually paid in monthly or quarterly installments. Choosing a medical plan that fits your needs and budget is based mostly on balance between deductible, coinsurance, and out-of-pocket limit.
What are the benefits of choosing a family plan over an individual plan?
Individual and family plans offer coverage to unemployed and self-employed people, students, people between jobs, and retirees who aren’t yet eligible for Medicare health insurance benefits. Under the Affordable Care Act (ACA), also known as Obamacare, individual and family plans are now sold through private, federal, and state online insurance exchanges. And, as before, they are also sold by brokers and insurance companies.
How should an international student navigate through healthcare plans?
International students on an F1 visa do not have any government related health insurance requirements to meet when studying in the USA. With this in mind, and because having adequate health insurance is so important, F1 visa holders and their dependents should follow the insurance guidelines set by their school.
